New Client Questionnaire

Rationale

When creating a new website, either from scratch or a redesign, you need to determine what your client is looking for. By asking a series of questions, you can determine the goals for the site and how it should look. Ideally, you would have your prospective client answer the Design questions ahead of time, and then you would talk through the remaining questions.

Questionnaire

    Is Client’s Project a Good Fit

  1. When do you need the website completed?
  2. What is your budget? Note: I don’t really ask this, but try to suss out the answer
  3. What are the technical requirements? Do I have the technical skills to create their website?
  4. What browsers must it run on? How about cell phones?

    5. Corporate Identity

  5. Company Name
  6. Tag Line
  7. Logo
  8. Domain name
  9. Phone Number
  10. Fax Number
  11. Address
  12. email addresses
  13. Days and hours of operation

    14. Short and Long-term Goals

  14. What are the immediate, short-term goals of your website? How will the website help your business?
  15. What are the long-term goals of your website? Will new pages or features need to be added to the website to accomplish the long-term goals?
  16. What is the purpose of your website?

    17. Content Questions

  17. List 5 (or more) adjectives that describe your company or should describe your company in order of relevance / importance.
  18. Are there any websites that you would consider your “competition”? What is the web address?
  19. What are your goals for the web site in terms of popularity? How many web visitors per month?
  20. What features should your website have? (calendar, forum, login, price comparison chart, contact form, blog, etc.?) For each feature, please state the goal of the feature.

    21. User Experience Questions

  21. If people are looking for you via a search engine (Google, Bing, Yahoo, etc.), what terms are they typing into the search field?
  22. When people come to your site, what are they looking for? What are they going to do?
  23. Why would they come back? If they do know you, why would they take the time to visit your site?
  24. List the top ten products and services you provide.
  25. Why are your products or services better than your competitions? What makes you unique?
  26. Describe your “average” visitor as best you can. What types of visitors do you want to get? Who is your target audience? (age, education, and other demographics? Job status? Economic status? Role in the community?
  27. What other visitors is your site going to get? Job seekers? Board members?
  28. Will web visitors have any special needs? (eyesight, language, mobility, reading level?) Are they technically savvy?

    29. Design Questions

  29. List 5 websites you like. Include the URL, what you like about each site, and what you would improve upon. Note: They do not need to be in the same industry as yours.
  30. List 5 websites you don’t like. Include the URL, what don’t you like about these sites? What redeeming qualities to they have?
  31. Do you have any definite remarks on what you don’t want to have on your website? (Flash, splash page, the color pink, a lot of text, etc.?)
  32. Anything else I should know?
